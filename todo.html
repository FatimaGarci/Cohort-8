AUSTRALIA

Sydney Opera House

The Opera House is a building that all ages can visit at any time. Simply walk along the water from Circular Quay.

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts centre at Sydney Harbour in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia. It is one of the 20th century's most famous and distinctive buildings.

Sydney Harbour Bridge

Starting at $143 for child and $168 for an adult, and ranging through to $388 for an adult dawn bridge climb.

The Sydney Harbour Bridge is an Australian heritage-listed steel through arch bridge across Sydney Harbour that carries rail, vehicular, bicycle, and pedestrian traffic between the Sydney central business district and the North Shore.

Darling Harbour

The area is free to visit, though many of its attractions charge an admission fee.

Darling Harbour is a harbour adjacent to the city centre of Sydney, New South Wales, Australia that is made up of a large recreational and pedestrian precinct that is situated on western outskirts of the Sydney central business district.

Great Barrier Reef

The daily fee to enter the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park itself is a measly A$6.50.

The Great Barrier Reef is one of the planet's most extraordinary natural wonders, its largest coral reef system and the only living organism that can be spotted from space. Protecting the reef is essential given its remarkable ecosystem and myriad marine life.

Twelve Apostles

The Twelve Apostlesis is a park all ages can visit at any time.

In Christian theology and ecclesiology, apostles, particularly the Twelve Apostles, were the primary disciples of Jesus according to the New Testament. In addition to Christian theology, Islamic theology also acknowledges the apostles in the Quran.

Great Ocean Road

The Great Ocean Road is visit from all ages at any time. No fee.

The Great Ocean Road is an Australian National Heritage listed 243-kilometre stretch of road along the south-eastern coast of Australia between the Victorian cities of Torquay and Allansford.
